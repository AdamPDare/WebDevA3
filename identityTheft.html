    What is Identity Theft

    Identity theft is where an individual steals someones personal information, to then use to commit fraudulent activities, usually to gain financially. This information could be: name, address, date of birth or even your bank details. People who do commit identity fraud do face very serious consequences, normally prison time.

    What are the common types of Identity Theft?

    Driver Licence Fraud. This occurs when someone registers/ is in possession of a driving licence under someone elses name. This then allows them to potentially commit various traffic or road violations, that could cause the person to lose their drivers licence.

    Financial Identity Theft. A criminal may have stolen someones personal information to then take control of your bank accounts or create ones under your name. If this does occur to someone, it takes a long time for them to rectify the damage that has been caused, such as: a poor credit score.

    Child Identity Theft. This type of identity theft invariably is commited by a relative and they will acquire a loan or credit card under their child's or relatives name. Since most children wont know what a band account is/ wont have a reason to check them criminals can exploit this and easily complete fraudulent activities.

    Change of Address. Fraudsters/ criminals can change your personal address to theirs so they receive your mail and online purchases (potentially) rather than you. This therefore enables them to see all your personal information, including your bank details.

    Employment Identity Theft. The unemployed, criminals and illegal immigrants may use stolen identification or personal information to acquire a job; hide their true personal information and job history from their employers.

    Methods of Identity Theft

    Theft of Physical Items

    Fraudsters/ Criminals may try to steal personal belongings or company equipment to obtain sensitive information about the individual or company. For example: stealing a company laptop or going through someones mail box to obtain bank statements. If they do obtain the information they are looking for, normally they will try and gain from it financially, so contact your bank or organisation immediately.

    To prevent equipment theft:

    • Encrypt your files on your device

    • Use passwords

    • Always lock your device when you leave it.

    • Shred bank statements when you do not need them

    • Never leave important items in plain sight, in a memorable place

    If you do lose a physical item such as a drivers licence or bank card contact the corresponding organisation immediately to prevent further damages to your reputation.

    Phishing

    Phishing is an illegal and fraudulent way to attempt and possibly obtain sensitive information such as: credit card details. Phishing can take the form of: E-mails or phone calls, pretending to be a trusted company.

    To prevent phishing emails and calls:

    1. Learn how a phishing scam looks/ research it. Maybe complete a course upon it.

    2. Never click upon the link in emails, unless from a trusted source. Same goes for downloading files from emails.

    3. Install anti-phishing add ons.

    4. Do not enter any information into an unsecure website.

    5. Change your password regurarly

    6. Regurarly update your software

    7. Install a firewall

    8. Ignore the pop ups

    Hacking

    Hacking is where someone attempts to gain unauthorised access to data, computer or system, invariably with malicious intent and to cause harm. They do this via exploting various weaknesses or security holes in a system or network, for example: using an algorithm to crack a password. In this case, it is with the goal to obtain sensitive information to then conduct fradulent activities.

    To prevent hackers from obtaining personal information:

    1. Encrypt your files

    2. Install a firewall and anti-virus

    3. Scan your device on a regular basis
